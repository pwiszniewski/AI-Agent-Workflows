## Research Plan: Finding "The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy"

This plan outlines the steps to identify, locate, and evaluate different versions of Douglas Adams' "The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy" using Wikipedia, Google, and YouTube.

### Phase 1: Metadata & Identification (Wikipedia)
**Goal:** Establish the exact details of the book to ensure the correct item is found.
*   **Search Terms:** "The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy (novel)"
*   **Key Tasks:**
    *   Confirm the author (Douglas Adams) and the original publication year (1979).
    *   Identify the book’s position in the series (e.g., Book 1 of the "trilogy of five").
    *   Note major publishers (e.g., Pan Books, Harmony Books) and ISBNs for various editions.
    *   Check for significant editions (e.g., 25th Anniversary, Illustrated Edition) to narrow down preferences.

### Phase 2: Location & Procurement (Google)
**Goal:** Find physical and digital retailers or repositories where the book is available.
*   **Search Terms:** 
    *   `"The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy" buy online`
    *   `"The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy" local library availability`
    *   `"The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy" ebook Kindle`
*   **Key Tasks:**
    *   **Commercial Search:** Identify pricing and availability on platforms like Amazon, Barnes & Noble, and AbeBooks (for used/collectible copies).
    *   **Library Search:** Use Google to find the book in local library networks or via digital lending services like Libby/OverDrive.
    *   **Format Verification:** Locate links for Hardcover, Paperback, E-book, and Audiobook formats.

### Phase 3: Visual & Audio Evaluation (YouTube)
**Goal:** Review specific editions and formats to decide which version to acquire.
*   **Search Terms:** 
    *   `"The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy" book review`
    *   `"The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy" illustrated edition flip-through`
    *   `"The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy" audiobook sample Stephen Fry vs Martin Freeman`
*   **Key Tasks:**
    *   **Edition Quality:** Watch "unboxing" or "bookshelf tour" videos to assess the print quality and illustrations of special editions.
    *   **Audiobook Selection:** Listen to narrator samples to determine a preference between the various famous recordings.
    *   **Content Summary:** Briefly watch a summary or "Should You Read" video to confirm the edition matches interest (e.g., the standalone novel vs. the "Ultimate" omnibus).

### Phase 4: Final Selection & Execution
**Goal:** Consolidate findings to make the final "find."
*   **Action:** Compare the price points found on Google with the aesthetic preferences found on YouTube and the bibliographic accuracy from Wikipedia.
*   **Output:** Select the specific ISBN/Format and proceed to the identified source (Library or Retailer) to acquire the book.
