# Research Plan: Locating 'The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy'

## Objective
To successfully identify, locate, and access a copy of 'The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy' by Douglas Adams.

## Phase 1: Confirm Bibliographic Details
- **Objective:** Obtain exact information to ensure the correct book is searched.
- **Actions:**
    - Identify the author: Douglas Adams.
    - Confirm title variants: Ensure the search accounts for variations (e.g., radio series vs. book).
    - Note common ISBNs (especially for popular editions or collections).

## Phase 2: Library Search
- **Objective:** Locate free-to-borrow physical or digital copies.
- **Actions:**
    - **WorldCat:** Use [WorldCat.org](https://www.worldcat.org/) to search for libraries worldwide that hold copies.
    - **Local Library Catalog:** Search your local library's website or app.
    - **Digital Lending:** Check availability on library-linked platforms like Libby or OverDrive for e-book and audiobook versions.

## Phase 3: Bookstore Search (Purchase)
- **Objective:** Find retailers selling new or used copies.
- **Actions:**
    - **Online Retailers:** Check major platforms (Amazon, Barnes & Noble, etc.) for new copies.
    - **Used Book Markets:** Search sites like AbeBooks, ThriftBooks, or Alibris for older or rare editions.
    - **Local Independent Bookstores:** Contact local shops to check stock or request an order.

## Phase 4: Digital Platforms (E-books & Audiobooks)
- **Objective:** Find instant-access digital formats.
- **Actions:**
    - **E-book Stores:** Search Kindle (Amazon), Kobo, or Apple Books.
    - **Audiobook Services:** Search Audible, Libro.fm, or Google Play Books.

## Phase 5: Verification & Accessibility
- **Objective:** Confirm format compatibility and language.
- **Actions:**
    - Verify that the edition found meets requirements (e.g., language, unabridged, specific narrators for audiobooks).
    - If a physical book is needed, verify current stock/check-out status before traveling.
